From Payouts to Payrolls: Corporate Payouts and the Real Economy

Job Market Paper

Corporate payouts have increased substantially in recent decades, sparking debate over their broader economic consequences. This paper examines the real effects of corporate payouts. Using IRS county-level dividend income data, I find that higher household dividend income leads to greater local job creation, particularly among small and young firms. I validate this finding using a shift-share instrument and a difference-in-differences design that exploits large special dividend payments in 2010Q4 and 2012Q4. The effect arises through higher consumer spending and expanded small-business lending fueled by increased bank deposits. Overall, this paper provides evidence that corporate payouts facilitate capital reallocation from mature public firms to the broader economy.

Shareholder Tax Cut, Financial Constraints, and Corporate Innovation

with Zhaozhao He and Jide Wintoki

Firms with different financial constraints exhibit heterogeneous responses to a shareholder tax cut: constrained firms increase innovation by issuing equity, as the tax cut lowers the cost of capital, whereas unconstrained firms do not expand investment but instead raise payouts.
